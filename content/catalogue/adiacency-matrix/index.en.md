A matrix-based network diagram. In connection-type network diagrams, the visibility of links can deteriorate significantly as the number of nodes increases (known as the hairball problem), but adjacency matrices do not suffer from this issue. However, they tend to require more display space compared to other chart types. Sorting the rows and columns by a particular data value can also make exploration more efficient.
